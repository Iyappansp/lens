Step-By-Step

How to Find Your Face Shape

Follow these simple steps in front of a mirror to identify your facial structure.

1

Measure the Width

Compare the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. See which area is the widest or if they are relatively equal.

2

Observe Jaw & Chin

Look at the angle of your jaw. Is it soft and rounded, sharp and square, or does it taper down to a pointed chin?

3

Determine Length

Compare your face's total height (forehead to chin) with its width (cheekbone to cheekbone). Oval and rectangular shapes are longer; round and square are wider/equal.

Perfect Fit Guide

Understanding Frame Sizes

Beyond shape, size matters for ultimate comfort and vision alignment. Look on the inside of your current frame temple arm to find three numbers (e.g., 52 ▢ 18 — 140):

  • Lens Width (52mm): The horizontal width of one lens.
  • Bridge Width (18mm): The distance between the two lenses over your nose.
  • Temple Length (140mm): The length of the frame arms from hinge to tip.
Complementary Elements

Hairstyle & Brow Compatibility Guide

Eyewear should harmonize not only with your face shape, but also with your hair structure and eyebrow line.

The Eyebrow Alignment Rule

The top rim of your frame should follow the curve of your eyebrows without covering them entirely or intersecting them awkwardly. Arched brows sync with curved round/oval frames, while straight brows pair best with rectangular, flat-top models.

Hair Volume & Frame Thickness

Thick, voluminous hair, heavy bangs, or long locks can easily handle thick acetate and oversized statement frames. If you have short hair, a shaved head, or fine hair, thinner metal or rimless frames will keep the focus on your features without overpowering them.

Skin Tone & Material Warmth

Cool skin tones (blue/pink undertones) shine with silver, gunmetal, clear, blue, and black frame materials. Warm skin tones (golden/peach undertones) are complemented by gold, honey, olive, warm tortoiseshell, and brown shades.
