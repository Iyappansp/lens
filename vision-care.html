Vision Care & Eye Health

Practical, expert-backed guidance to help you protect your eyes at every stage of life.

Daily Habits

Eye Health Tips

Small daily habits make a measurable difference in long-term eye health.

  • Follow the 20-20-20 rule during screen time
  • Stay hydrated to support tear film health
  • Eat foods rich in omega-3s and leafy greens
  • Get adequate sleep to support eye recovery
Screen Time

Digital Eye Strain

Extended screen use can cause dryness, blurred vision, and headaches. Blue light filtering lenses and mindful breaks help significantly.

  • Position screens at arm's length, slightly below eye level
  • Reduce blue light exposure with filtering lenses
  • Blink consciously — screen use reduces blink rate

For Growing Eyes

Children's Vision

Early detection of vision issues supports learning and development. We recommend a first eye exam by age 3, then annually through school years.

  • Watch for squinting, head-tilting, or sitting too close to screens
  • Annual exams catch myopia progression early
  • Durable, flexible frames suit active kids
Outdoor Protection

UV Protection

UV exposure contributes to long-term eye damage, including cataracts. Quality sunglasses and UV-coated lenses are essential, year-round.

  • Choose lenses with 100% UVA/UVB protection
  • Polarized lenses reduce glare from reflective surfaces
  • UV exposure is high even on cloudy days
